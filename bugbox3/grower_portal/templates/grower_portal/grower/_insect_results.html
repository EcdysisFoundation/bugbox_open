We summarize your insect sampling in three ways: abundance (total insects counted), species richness (distinct morphospecies identified), and common species (Hill H2): a diversity index that reflects how evenly individuals are spread across those morphospecies. Think of H2 as an estimate of how many species are common in your field.

Combined puts all insects from all your sites into one group and calculates a single value. For common species (H2), that number is neither the total nor the average of the per-site values: it reflects how common species are across your whole farm that year.
